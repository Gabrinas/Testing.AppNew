To fully utilise this recording and get the expected recording, kindly take note of the following instructions:

1. Select one sentence at a time from the transcript page.
2. Ensure your accent is properly taking care of in the recording.
3. Ensure you are cautious of the pace at which you pronounce each word in the sentence.
Do not be too fast and do not be too slow.
4. Ensure your recorded words match that provided in the transcript index chosen. If you mistakenly
deviate, kindly reload the page to start over.
5. Full pronunciation of all punctuation marks should be carried out e.g. you say "comma", "full-stop",
"hyphen" etc. You also pronounce bracket as "open baracket" or "close bracket"
6. Exception to rule 5 is to pronounce words e.g. kg/m3 as "kilogram per meter cube" and not "kilogram slash meter cube."
7. Abbreviations should be pronounced in full. e.g. mmHg should be pronounced as "millimeters of mercury",
For the clinical domain data, words using the most known pronunciations by people. e.g.
CT should be pronounced as "see tee" and not as "Computed Tomography",
"CXR" should be pronounced "Chest Xray" and not "see ex arr"

8. For saving of audio file, if a file is saved with "TEDTALK_PARTA_3_55", after recording, to save such file, use the
tag name + "_ENG"
9. For the instance, for the tag above, the text file is picked inside the folder
"TED_TALK_MALARIA_ENGLISH_TRANSCRIPT_PARTA_3" so, it is an English text file, it is saved with
"TEDTALK_PARTA_3_55_ENG. .
10. Ensure you select the exact main folder i.e. public health or general domain folder, and
that you store the English audio recorded in the allocated folder particular,
paying attention to TED_TALK_MALARIA_ENGLISH_TRANSCRIPT, _PARTA, and _2 for the case of
TED_TALK_MALARIA_ENGLISH_TRANSCRIPT_PARTA_2 as the name of a sub-folder.

11. Upload your recordings using the link Upload Audio Files while simultaneously submit
the details of your recordings using the form on the top-right of this page or
the Google form provided on the bottom-right of this page.
12. Record a maximum of 2.5 hours and take a break every 30 minutes of recordings.

Also submit the following details per audio recording:
1. File name you save the recording with.
2. The type of data you just recorded (either general or public health)
3. Duration (in seconds) of each of your recordings
4. Accent type utilised for the recording.
